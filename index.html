1. Rule of 20-20-20
Digital eye strain can become a problem for people who need to focus on a computer screen all day while working.

The 20-20-20 rule helps ease digital eye strain. The rule is easy:
  • A person needs to look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds every 20 minutes while working on a computer.
    • 2. Change the focus

    The focus change exercise can also help with digital eye strain. People should perform this exercise while sitting.

    1. Hold a finger few inches away from one eye.
    2. Focus on the finger.
    3. Move finger slowly away from the face.
    4. Focus on an object farther away, and then back on the finger.
    5. Bring the finger back closer to the eye.
    6. Focus on an object farther away.
    7. Repeat three times.
    3. Movements
    This eye movement exercise also helps with digital eye strain.

  • Close the eyes. Slowly move the eyes upward, then downward. Continue for 15 seconds.
  • Slowly move the eyes to the left, then to the right. Continue for 15 seconds.
  • Slowly move the eyes to Clockwise direction. Continue for 15 seconds.
  • Slowly move the eyes to Anti - Clockwise direction. Continue for 15 seconds.
    • 4. Draw figures
    The figure 8, circle, rectangle etc exercise can also help ease digital eye strain.

  • Focus on an area on the floor around 8 feet away.
  • Move the eyes in the shape of a figure 8 or anything.
  • Trace the imaginary figure for 30 seconds, then switch direction.
    • 5. Pencil pushups
    A doctor might recommend this for vision therapy.

  • Hold a pencil at armâ€™s length, at center the eyes.
  • While Looking at pencil try to keep single image of it while slowly moving it toward the nose.
  • Move the pencil toward the nose until the pencil is no longer a single image.
  • Position the pencil at the closest point where it is still a single image.
  • Repeat the process for 30 seconds
    • 6. Brock string
    This helps improve eye coordination.

    • A person needs a long string & some colored beads.
    • Tie one end of the string to a still object or another person can hold it.
    • Hold the other end of the string just below the nose.
    • Place one bead on the string.
    • Look straight at the bead with both eyes open.

      • If eyes are working correctly: A person should see the bead and two strings in the shape of an X.
      • If one eye is closed: One of the strings will disappear, which means that the eye is suppressing.
      • If the person sees two beads and two strings, the eyes are not converged at the bead.
